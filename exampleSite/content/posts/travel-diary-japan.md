A collection of memories from my recent trip to Japan.

## Tokyo

The city that never sleeps. From the neon-lit streets of Shibuya to the serene gardens of Meiji Shrine, Tokyo is a city of contrasts.

### Shibuya Crossing

Standing at the busiest pedestrian crossing in the world is an experience like no other. Hundreds of people cross from every direction in a perfectly choreographed chaos.

### Tsukiji Outer Market

Fresh sushi for breakfast. Grilled eel, tamagoyaki, and matcha ice cream. A food lover's paradise.

## Kyoto

The cultural heart of Japan. Temples, gardens, and geishas.

> "In Kyoto, feeling the seasons is not just a pastime — it's a way of life."

### Fushimi Inari

Thousands of vermilion torii gates winding up the mountainside. I walked the full trail — it took over two hours but was absolutely worth it.

### Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Walking through the towering bamboo stalks feels like entering another world.

## Osaka

Known for its food and friendly people. Osaka has a different energy from Tokyo — more relaxed, more down-to-earth.

### Dotonbori

The street food capital of Japan. Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, and kushikatsu on every corner.

## What I Learned

Travel changes you. It opens your mind to new perspectives and reminds you how big the world really is.
